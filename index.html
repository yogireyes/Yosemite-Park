Yosemite Falls

One of the tallest waterfalls in North America, consisting of Upper Yosemite Fall, Middle Cascades, and Lower Yosemite Fall. It's a must-see for its impressive beauty and magnitude.

Half Dome

An iconic granite formation that attracts adventurers and hikers. Whether you hike to its summit or view it from below, Half Dome is a symbol of Yosemite and a captivating sight.

El Capitan:

A massive granite monolith known for its rock climbing challenges and stunning views. It's a prominent feature of the Yosemite landscape and an awe-inspiring sight for visitors.

Tunnel View:

Scenic overlook offering a breathtaking panoramic view of Yosemite Valley, including El Capitan, Half Dome, and Bridalveil Fall. It provides a stunning introduction to Yosemite

Why Visit?

Visit Yosemite for its stunning waterfalls, iconic granite formations, and breathtaking views at Tunnel View.

Get Inspired
Yosemite is inspiring due to its awe-inducing natural beauty, showcasing towering waterfalls, towering cliffs, and picturesque landscapes that evoke a sense of wonder and reverence for the natural world
Gain The Knowledge
Yosemite is considered knowledgeable due to its rich geological and ecological diversity, providing valuable insights into Earth's natural processes, ecosystems, and environmental preservation, making it a significant site for scientific research and educational purposes.
Open Your Mind
Yosemite has the power to open minds by immersing individuals in its vast, unspoiled wilderness, offering a profound connection with nature that fosters contemplation, appreciation, and a broader perspective on the world and our place within it.

Meet The Community

Yosemite is home to diverse wildlife, including black bears, deer, eagles, and other fascinating creatures inhabiting its lush forests and skies.

Black Bear

The black bear, Ursus americanus, is an adaptable mammal known for its black/brown fur and love for foraging in Yosemite's diverse ecosystem.

Mule Dear

The mule deer, Odocoileus hemionus, is a graceful herbivore characterized by large ears, distinctive antlers, and a gentle demeanor in Yosemite's landscapes.

Bob Cat

The bobcat, Lynx rufus, is a stealthy, solitary wildcat recognized by its short tail, tufted ears, and superb hunting skills in Yosemite's habitats

Mountain Lion

The mountain lion, Puma concolor, is a powerful, elusive big cat known for hunting prowess and sleek body, vital to Yosemite's ecological balance.
